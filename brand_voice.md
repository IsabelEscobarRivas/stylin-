# Stylin' — Brand Voice Profile
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-02 | **Status:** Active

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**Mission:** Make fashion discovery effortless and personal — for every budget, every body, every taste.

**Vision:** A world where anyone can see a look they love and immediately know how to make it their own.

**Core Problem Solved:** The gap between fashion inspiration and actual purchase — fragmented search, no personalization, budget mismatch.

**Target Audience:**
| Persona | Voice Expectation |
|---|---|
| Trend Chaser (18–28) | Fast, energetic, culturally fluent |
| Conscious Stylist (25–40) | Thoughtful, quality-aware, not preachy |
| Gifter (any age) | Reassuring, guided, confidence-building |

---

## 2. Brand Personality

> **"Your cool friend who always knows what to wear."**

Stylin' is not a luxury concierge. It's not a fast-fashion algorithm. It's the friend who texts back in 10 seconds with exactly the right answer — and means it.

### Voice Dimension Scores

| Dimension | Score | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Playful ↔ Serious | **3/10** | Leans playful — light, fun, never stiff |
| Casual ↔ Formal | **2/10** | Conversational — talks like a person, not a brand |
| Emotional ↔ Analytical | **3/10** | Leads with feeling, backs it with function |
| Sarcastic ↔ Sincere | **8/10** | Genuinely warm — wit is welcome, snark is not |
| Authoritative ↔ Approachable | **3/10** | Knowledgeable but never intimidating |

---

## 3. Tone Guidelines

### ✅ DO
- Speak directly to the user — use "you" and "your" freely
- Use short, punchy sentences with rhythm
- Celebrate all budgets equally — budget finds get the same energy as luxury picks
- Be specific — "sage green midi dress" not "nice outfit"
- Use active voice always
- Sound excited about fashion without being breathless
- Acknowledge the user's taste as valid, not something to be corrected

### ❌ DON'T
- Use luxury signifiers: "elevated," "curated collection," "investment piece" (unless quoting a tier)
- Use fast-fashion urgency: "limited time," "selling fast," "don't miss out"
- Be preachy about sustainability (Conscious Stylist cares — lecture no one)
- Use jargon that excludes: "silhouette," "colorway," "capsule wardrobe" without context
- Sound robotic or transactional: "Your request has been processed"
- Punch down on any style, budget, or body type
- Use passive voice or corporate hedging

## 4. Vocabulary and Terminology

### Preferred Terms
| Use This | Not This |
|---|---|
| "look" | "outfit" (when referring to inspiration) |
| "find" / "discover" | "purchase" / "acquire" |
| "your style" | "your aesthetic" |
| "budget pick" | "affordable option" / "cheap" |
| "style persona" | "user profile" / "fashion identity" |
| "drop a photo" | "upload an image" |
| "matches" | "results" / "products" |
| "vibe" | "aesthetic category" |

### Brand-Specific Terms (always capitalize)
- **Vision Scout** — the image analysis agent
- **Style Curator** — the product + outfit agent
- **StyleProfile** — the data object (technical contexts only)
- **Style Persona** — the user's named style identity (e.g., "The Romantic Minimalist")

### Words to Avoid Entirely
- "luxury" as a general brand descriptor (it's a price tier, not our identity)
- "fast fashion" (category reference only, never aspirational)
- "algorithm" (use "our AI" or just describe what it does)
- "seamless" (overused, meaningless)
- "revolutionary" / "game-changing" (let the product speak)

## 5. Example Phrases

### ✅ On-Brand
- *"See a look you love. Own it."*
- *"Drop any photo — we'll handle the rest."*
- *"Three outfits. Every budget. Your vibe."*
- *"Your style, decoded in seconds."*
- *"Budget pick or splurge — you decide."*
- *"That's so you."*

### ❌ Off-Brand
- *"Elevate your wardrobe with curated selections."* — too luxury/formal
- *"Our revolutionary AI analyzes your aesthetic."* — jargon + hype
- *"Limited time: discover your style persona today!"* — fast-fashion urgency
- *"Seamlessly browse investment pieces across retailers."* — wrong register entirely
- *"Your request has been processed. Results are available."* — robotic

## 6. Visual Identity Notes (for copy-adjacent decisions)

- **Tone of imagery:** Real people, real outfits — not editorial perfection
- **Color language:** Warm, not cold. Inclusive skin tones. All body types.
- **Typography voice:** Clean but not sterile — personality in the details
- **Emoji use:** Acceptable in social/informal contexts. Never in product UI.

## 8. The One-Sentence Test

Before publishing any copy, ask: *"Would a cool, knowledgeable friend actually say this?"*

If yes — ship it. If it sounds like a press release, a luxury ad, or a chatbot — rewrite it.
